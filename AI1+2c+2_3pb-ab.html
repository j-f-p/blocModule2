A Constructive Existence Proof of a Big O Statement


Abstract

This article defines big O notation, suitable for analyzing the asymptotic behavior of the computational complexity of an algorithm that processes a data set of size $n$. Then, it proves a statement involving the notation.

Big O Notation

Let $n$ be a positive integer and $T(n)$ be a positive real-valued function. Then, $T(n) = O(f(n))$ if and only if there exist a positive real number $c$ and a positive integer $n_0$ such that $$ T(n) \leq c \cdot f(n) \quad \forall n \geq n_0 $$

Statement

$$ 2^{\sqrt{\log_2 n}} = O(\sqrt{n}) $$

Proof

By the definition of big O notation, the statement is equivalent to the double existential statement $$ (\exists\ c \in \mathbb{R}^+)(\exists\ n_0 \in \mathbb{Z}^+) (2^{\sqrt{\log_2 n}} \leq c \cdot \sqrt{n} \quad \forall n \geq n_0) $$ Arrive at a constructive existence proof by analyzing the expressions of the inequality algebraically. Consider that
$\quad\enspace \sqrt{n} = 2^{\log_2 \sqrt{n}} $
and
$\quad\enspace \log_2 \sqrt{n} = 0.5 \log_2 n = 0.5 \sqrt{\log_2 n} \sqrt{\log_2 n} $
Furthermore,
$\quad\enspace 0.5 \sqrt{\log_2 n} \sqrt{\log_2 n} > 0.5 \sqrt{\log_2 n} \quad \forall \sqrt{\log_2 n} > 1 $
Note
$\quad\enspace \sqrt{\log_2 n} > 1 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \log_2 n > 1 \quad\Rightarrow\quad n > 2$
and
$\quad\enspace 0.5 \sqrt{\log_2 n} > \sqrt{\log_2 n} $
Thus,
$\quad\enspace 0.5 \sqrt{\log_2 n} \sqrt{\log_2 n} > \sqrt{\log_2 n} \quad \forall n > 2 $
Therefore,
$\quad\enspace \log_2 \sqrt{n} > \sqrt{\log_2 n} \quad \forall n > 2 $
or
$\quad\enspace \sqrt{\log_2 n} < \log_2 \sqrt{n} \quad \forall n > 2 $
Thus,
$\quad\enspace 2^{\sqrt{\log_2 n}} < 2^{\log_2 \sqrt{n}} \quad \forall n > 2 $
Therefore,
$\quad\enspace 2^{\sqrt{\log_2 n}} < \sqrt{n} \quad \forall n > 2 $
Hence,
$\quad\enspace 2^{\sqrt{\log_2 n}} < \sqrt{n} \quad \forall n \geq 3 $
Clearly, $c = 1$ and $n_0 = 3$ satisfy the existential statement. Therefore, it is true that $ 2^{\sqrt{\log_2 n}} = O(\sqrt{n}). \quad\blacksquare$
